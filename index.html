I am a Master's student and Graduate Research Assistant in the Department of Computer Science at Colorado State University, where I work in the SIGNAL Lab under the supervision of Dr. Bruce Draper. My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, computer vision, and medical image analysis, with a focus on building reliable and interpretable AI systems for healthcare applications.

My current work focuses on ultrasound imaging of the lower limb, particularly on the detection of vascular structures from ultrasound images. This research combines image analysis, pattern recognition, and learning-based methods to support accurate identification of clinically relevant structures in challenging imaging conditions. I am especially interested in developing methods that are not only effective in prediction, but also transparent and understandable in how they reach their decisions.

More broadly, I am interested in medical imaging problems where robust visual understanding can contribute to diagnostic support and clinical decision-making. My work is guided by the idea that, in healthcare settings, AI systems must be both accurate and interpretable in order to be useful in practice.
